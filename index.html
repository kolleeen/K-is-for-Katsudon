Katsudon (カツ丼)

Katsudon is a Japanese rice bowl that is famous in Japan, along with other countries as well! It is also fulfilling and full of flavors! The name "Katsudon" comes from two dishes: Tonkatsu, a deep-fried and breaded pork cutlet, and Donburi, a rice bowl dish of meat and vegetables.



The Origin of the Dish

While there are many theories about the origin of Katsudon, a local newspaper back in 1995 said that it began in a restaurant near Kōfu, Yamanashi. Okumura Main Store, a long-established store, began offering deep-fried pork cutlet donburi in the late 1890s. Also, another article said that Katsudon was already existing even during the Meiji era. For this reason, the the said theory is the oldest confirmed information at this time.


Other Theories on the Origin

Waseda Theory

After returning from Germany, Masutaro Takahata opened a store in front of Waseda University. He sold Katsudon to university students which became a word of mouth.

Osaka Theory

A student of Waseda High School by the name of Keijiro Nakanishi invented egg-bound Katsudon. He also sold the dish in Osaka first.

Sancho-an Theory

Some people believe that it was Nakanishi of now closed Sancho-an who created this worldwide famous rice bowl dish. They believe that his store, Sancho-an, is the first soba restaurant to offer Katsudon.
